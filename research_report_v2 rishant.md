# CHAPTER 1: INTRODUCTION

__1.1 Background of the Study__

In today's digitally connected world, social media has evolved from a simple communication tool into one of the most powerful marketing platforms for businesses of all sizes. Social media includes platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, WhatsApp, Twitter, and Snapchat, through which individuals create, share, and exchange information, ideas, and experiences in virtual communities and networks. Evans (2008) defined social media as a medium of communication where people with the same minds connect and interact with each other to share their life experiences.

The rapid adoption of social media technology has provided businesses with entirely new ways to market products and reach consumers. As the use of social media has increased, so has the use of social media marketing (SMM) by firms as a core business strategy. Social media marketing returns consumers to the focal point of the business world and gives marketers a new set of instruments to connect with customers and integrate them into brands through creative ways. It is estimated that more than 500 million individuals interact with social media globally (Ostrow, 2010), and this number has grown exponentially in subsequent years.

Initially, brands marketed their products through traditional methods such as newspapers, magazines, radio, and television. These tools informed consumers about the availability of products but offered limited scope for personalisation or two-way communication. In contrast, social media marketing enables brands to engage directly with consumers, receive real-time feedback, and create personalised content that resonates with specific target audiences. Social media has made business environments more transparent, as it reveals both positive and negative aspects of a brand that can spread rapidly through shares and reposts.

Brands create pages on social networking sites where they share product insights through videos, images, and articles. Content marketing has emerged as a key strategy within social media marketing, whereby companies create and distribute valuable, consistent, and pertinent information to attract and retain a clearly defined audience. According to the Content Marketing Institute (2015), the objective of content marketing is to drive profitable customer action. Electronic word of mouth (eWOM), influencer endorsements, and social media advertising are further dimensions through which social media marketing influences consumer perceptions and purchase decisions.

Jhansi, a historically significant city in Uttar Pradesh, India, has witnessed a notable rise in social media usage among its population, particularly among students and young professionals. Local businesses in Jhansi are increasingly using platforms like Instagram, YouTube, and WhatsApp to promote their products and services. Understanding the extent to which social media marketing influences consumer purchase decisions in this context is therefore both timely and relevant. This study was conducted with 43 respondents from the Jhansi region and aims to assess the impact of social media marketing on consumer purchase decisions, with a specific focus on the local market context.

__1.2 Statement of the Problem__

Despite the widespread adoption of social media marketing by businesses, there remains a need to empirically understand how different dimensions of social media marketing specifically influence the purchase decisions of consumers, particularly in smaller Indian cities like Jhansi. While global research has established a positive link between social media marketing and consumer behaviour, local-level studies examining the specific role of influencer content, customer reviews, brand pages, discount offers, and paid advertisements on consumer decisions are limited. This study addresses this gap by collecting and analysing primary data from 43 respondents in Jhansi to examine the relationship between social media marketing and consumer purchase decisions.

__1.3 Objectives of the Study__

The objectives of this study are:

- To examine the role of social media marketing in influencing consumer purchase decisions.
- To assess the effectiveness of different types of social media content in driving purchase behaviour.
- To evaluate consumer trust in social media product reviews and influencer endorsements.
- To analyse the effectiveness of local Jhansi-based businesses in using social media for marketing.
- To test the hypothesis that social media marketing has a significant positive impact on consumer purchase decisions.

__1.4 Research Questions__

- Does social media marketing significantly influence consumer purchase decisions?
- Which type of social media content has the greatest influence on consumers?
- Do consumers trust product reviews and influencer endorsements on social media?
- Are local businesses in Jhansi effectively utilising social media for marketing?

__1.5 Significance of the Study__

This study is significant for businesses, marketers, and policymakers in understanding the growing role of social media marketing in shaping consumer behaviour. For local businesses in Jhansi, the findings provide actionable insights into how social media can be used more effectively to reach and convert consumers. For academic researchers, this study contributes primary empirical data on social media marketing and consumer purchase decisions from a small-city Indian context, which is underrepresented in existing literature. The study also provides a foundation for future research in regional digital marketing behaviour.

__1.6 Scope of the Study__

This study focuses on consumers who are active users of social media platforms in Jhansi and surrounding areas. The study is limited to 43 respondents and examines social media marketing dimensions including brand awareness, content marketing, influencer endorsements, customer reviews, and social media advertising. The study uses a quantitative research design and analyses data through frequency tables and percentage analysis. The findings are particularly relevant to the youth demographic (18-25 years), which constitutes the majority of the sample.

# CHAPTER 2: REVIEW OF LITERATURE

__2.1 Social Media and Social Media Marketing__

Social media is a platform that allows users to create and exchange content with each other. It is a group of applications built on the technical basis of Web 2.0 (Whiting & Williams, 2013). Social media encompasses platforms such as Facebook, Instagram, YouTube, Twitter, WhatsApp, and Snapchat, which facilitate online communication, sharing of information, and social interaction. Social media is considered to be one of the biggest changes since the industrial revolution (Phillips, 2013), fundamentally altering how individuals communicate and how businesses reach their markets.

Social media marketing (SMM) is defined as the use of social media platforms to connect with audiences, build brand awareness, increase sales, and drive website traffic. Social media marketing described as a connection between brands and consumers, while providing a way for consumers for social interaction and centred networking (Chi, 2011). As people are spending more time on the internet, especially on social media sites, brands are now investing in digital marketing more than in traditional marketing (Baines & Fill, 2014). Companies gain competitive advantages through social media analytics by assessing competitor strategies, supplier performance, and consumer behaviour (Fan and Gordon, 2014).

Social media marketing encompasses several dimensions that influence consumer behaviour: (a) Social Media Advertising, involving paid promotions on platforms; (b) Content Marketing, which includes brand pages, videos, and blog posts; (c) Influencer Marketing, involving endorsements by social media creators; (d) Electronic Word of Mouth (eWOM), comprising user reviews and peer recommendations; and (e) Social Community Management, involving brand interaction with followers. Each of these dimensions plays a role in shaping consumer perceptions and purchase intentions.

Research by Sharma (2020) on 220 respondents found a positive significant linear relationship between Social Media Marketing and Consumer Buying Decision Making, with a Pearson correlation coefficient of 0.556 (significant at 1% level). The study found that e-word of mouth had the highest mean value (3.66) among SMM dimensions, followed by social community (3.50) and social media advertising (3.205). Furthermore, a study by Ali et al. (2016) on 145 respondents using multiple linear regression confirmed that social media marketing has a significant positive impact on consumer perception, with a regression equation showing social media marketing contributing 0.390 to consumer perception.

__2.2 Brand Awareness__

Brand awareness refers to consumers' consciousness about the brand and the products offered by it. It is the most significant thing for any company to provide the best possible information about their brand and its offerings to attract potential consumers. Brand awareness through social media plays a major role in increasing market shares for brands, as it is an influential tool for consumer repetitive buying behaviour. Brands must use different tactics to keep their target audiences engaged through post updates, including images, videos, and articles.

Nowadays, companies are experimenting with different tools to interact with their current and potential customers. Hashtags, for instance, allow companies to display their ideas and concepts and increase their page visits and brand awareness (Daeun Kim, 2017). Celebrity endorsement is one of the factors that can help in creating awareness for a brand in the market. There are certain dimensions to measure the credibility of a celebrity that include trustworthiness, familiarity, and expertise to positively influence brand awareness. Celebrities, being public figures, can work as a bridge in connecting individuals with a brand (Kim, Choeb, & Petrick, 2018).

Active consumers get engaged with brands on social media and enhance the brand page views, which leads to greater brand awareness (Hutter et al., 2018). The brands can enhance their brand awareness by stimulating virtual interactivity, offering rewards, and providing better information systems (Barreda et al., 2015). Research by Ansari et al. (2019) found that brand awareness has a weak but positive significant relationship with consumer purchase decision (r = 0.406), with a standardized Beta of 0.213, confirming that brand awareness significantly contributes to purchase decision making.

__2.3 Content Marketing__

According to the Content Marketing Institute, content marketing is a marketing technique of creating and distributing valuable, relevant, and consistent content to attract and acquire a clearly defined audience, with the objective of driving profitable customer action. Content seeks to answer queries, assisting consumers in resolving their problems and facilitating the decision-making process, rather than focusing solely on the brand or company (Templeman, 2015).

The key purposes of content marketing include customer perspective reinforcement, sales and lead generation, consumer engagement, brand awareness creation, customer upsells, and building enthusiastic followers, fans, and subscribers (Pulizzi, 2012). Content can take the form of blogs, videos, case studies, infographics, e-books, images, and professional reviews. Videos are considered to be the most effective marketing technique for sharing company insights with consumers. The broadly used content marketing techniques by B2C companies are social media networks, website articles, blogs, and vlogs (North America Report, 2014).

Content marketing works as a relationship-building technique through which customers can associate themselves with brands. If the content is strong and engaging, it leads consumers to purchase decisions. Companies should work on the quality of the content rather than quantity, since it is the content that encourages customers to make a purchase decision (Puro, 2013). Ansari et al. (2019) found that social media content marketing has the strongest positive relationship with consumer purchase decision among all predictors (r = 0.573, β = 0.488), establishing it as the most influential dimension of social media marketing for driving purchases.

__2.4 Consumer Purchase Decision__

The purchase decision is the decision-making process by consumers regarding a transaction of goods or services being offered in the market. Consumer buying decisions are influenced by perceptions, motivations, learning, attitudes and beliefs (Lamb et al., 2010). The brand image and quality of the product are considered to be the main factors in contributing to consumer purchase intention, along with reviews and feedback from those who have already experienced the product (Gogoi, 2013).

The consumer buying decision-making (CBDM) process involves several sequential stages: need recognition, information search, evaluation of alternatives, purchase decision, and post-purchase behaviour. Social media has become a powerful tool at each stage of this process. Consumers use social media to search for product information (mean = 4.01 in Sharma's study), evaluate alternatives through comparison platforms, and make final purchase decisions influenced by social proof and peer recommendations. With the change of time and trends, consumers tend to watch vlogger reviews before making purchase decisions (Daeun Kim, 2017).

Dehghani and Tumer (2015) considered brand image and brand equity as the two main factors for determining purchase intention of buyers, in their study on Facebook effectiveness. The virtual communities of brands on social media generate consumer participation and trust, which is developed through information and experience sharing by users and leads to purchase desire. According to Hajli (2014), the independent variables for purchase decision include eWOM, brand loyalty, and social media marketing, all of which are positively associated with consumer purchase intentions.

__2.5 Electronic Word of Mouth (eWOM)__

Electronic word of mouth (eWOM) is a statement made by consumers on social networking platforms about a product or service, which can be either positive or negative. Research shows that reviews given by friends or users on social media put twice as much trust in consumers' minds as the brand's own marketing campaign (Ludwig et al., 2013). A study conducted on online retailing found that about 73% of respondents agreed that brands' consumer reviews and comments regarding a product help them overcome concerns and encourage them in making online purchases (Mintel, 2015).

User-generated content (UGC) tends to be more productive in connecting with consumers, where they can contact, engage, express themselves, and contribute to creating content (Huotari et al., 2015). UGC helps in building virtual communities for brands, which eventually enhances brand image as well (Holliman & Rowley, 2014). Young consumers are more actively participating in sharing their views regarding products or services they have experienced. Blogging is observed to be the most effective form of eWOM from a marketing point of view (Hsu et al., 2013).

Consumers rely on friends' suggestions about products on social networks. A healthy content strategy by the brand can help in retaining customers who can become brand advocates in the future, helping the company attract the potential target market by posting reviews and spreading positive word of mouth (Hur et al., 2011). In the context of this study, the trust factor in social media reviews is a key variable, given that 72.1% of respondents in the primary data expressed a neutral stance on trusting product reviews on social media, indicating scope for brands to build greater credibility.

# CHAPTER 3: RESEARCH METHODOLOGY

__3.1 Research Design__

The research type adopted for this study is quantitative research, which focuses on numerical results and statistical analysis of data. A descriptive research design has been employed, which is particularly suited to studying relationships between variables and describing the characteristics of phenomena. The study collects primary data through a structured online questionnaire and analyses it using frequency tables, percentage analysis, and descriptive statistics.

__3.2 Population and Sample Size__

The population of this study comprises social media users in Jhansi, Uttar Pradesh, who actively engage with brand content on various social networking platforms. As the exact population size of social media users in Jhansi is not defined, a sample-based approach has been adopted. A total of 43 respondents participated in the study. While this sample is relatively small, it provides meaningful preliminary insights into consumer attitudes and behaviours in the Jhansi region. The sample includes respondents from various age groups and educational backgrounds, with a predominance of young adults (18-25 years) who are the most active social media users.

__3.3 Sampling Technique__

Convenience sampling technique was employed to collect data from respondents. An online survey questionnaire was distributed through social media platforms (Google Forms via WhatsApp and Instagram) to reach a diverse set of respondents in Jhansi and surrounding areas. Convenience sampling was considered appropriate because the study targets active social media users, who are readily accessible through digital platforms. This technique allowed for rapid data collection within a defined geographic and demographic context.

__3.4 Research Instrument__

The research instrument used is a structured online questionnaire comprising 17 questions across three sections. Section I covers demographic information (age, gender, educational qualification). Section II covers social media usage patterns (platform preference, daily usage hours, frequency of ad exposure, and type of content most influential). Section III comprises Likert-scale and multiple-choice questions measuring attitudes toward social media marketing and its influence on purchase decisions. The questionnaire was originally designed for the study and distributed via Google Forms between 30 March and 31 March 2026.

__3.5 Data Collection__

Primary data for this study was collected through an online questionnaire survey distributed on Google Forms. The survey was shared via social media platforms to respondents in the Jhansi region. A total of 43 valid responses were received within two days (30-31 March 2026). All responses were recorded electronically with timestamps and analysed directly from the Google Sheets export. Respondent email addresses were collected only for survey administration purposes and have been excluded from analysis to protect confidentiality.

__3.6 Data Analysis Tools__

The data collected was analysed using Microsoft Excel for frequency counting and percentage computation. The following analyses were conducted:

- Frequency and Percentage Analysis: Computed for all demographic and survey variables to understand distribution patterns.
- Descriptive Analysis: Mean responses were computed for Likert-scale items to assess central tendencies.
- Hypothesis Testing: Based on the frequency distributions and dominant response patterns, hypotheses were tested using a threshold of majority response (more than 50% of respondents agreeing with a statement indicates hypothesis acceptance).

# CHAPTER 5: FINDINGS, CONCLUSIONS AND RECOMMENDATIONS

__5.1 Major Findings__

The following major findings have emerged from the analysis of primary data collected from 43 respondents in the Jhansi region:

- Demographic Profile: The majority of respondents (72.1%) are in the 18-25 age group, with a nearly equal gender split (51.2% female, 48.8% male). Undergraduates constitute 65.1% of the sample, reflecting a young, educated social media user base.
- Platform Preference: Instagram (27.9%) and YouTube (25.6%) are the most preferred social media platforms, followed by multiple-platform combinations involving WhatsApp and Snapchat. These platforms are the primary channels through which social media marketing reaches consumers.
- Daily Usage: 97.7% of respondents spend at least 1 hour per day on social media, with 46.5% spending 1-2 hours and 37.2% spending 3-4 hours, indicating high daily exposure to social media content.
- Ad Exposure: 88.4% of respondents encounter advertisements on social media sometimes, often, or very often, confirming the wide reach of social media advertising among the target demographic.
- Most Influential Content: Influencer/creator posts are the most influential type of social media content (39.5%), followed by customer reviews and testimonials (20.9%), and brand pages and official posts (18.6%). Paid advertisements are the least influential (9.3%).
- Product Discovery: 58.1% of respondents agree that social media advertisements help them discover new products and services, supporting the role of social media in the awareness stage of the consumer decision journey.
- Trust in Reviews: 72.1% of respondents are neutral about trusting social media product reviews, indicating that consumer trust in social media content remains moderate and needs to be built through authentic and verified content.
- Actual Purchase Behaviour: 60.5% of respondents have made a purchase after seeing a product on social media (9.3% many times and 51.2% once or twice), and a further 32.6% have at least considered it. Only 7.0% have never made or considered a purchase based on social media content.
- Price Comparison: 62.8% agree that social media marketing helps them compare prices and make better purchasing decisions, highlighting social media's role as an information and evaluation tool.
- Influencer Endorsements: 44.2% agree that influencer endorsements affect their buying choices, with 41.9% neutral, indicating moderate but meaningful influencer marketing impact.
- Local Business Effectiveness: 76.8% consider local Jhansi businesses to be somewhat or very effective on social media. However, only 39.5% have actually purchased from a local Jhansi business after seeing their social media promotion, indicating a conversion gap.
- Overall Social Media Influence: While 37.2% consciously agree that social media influences their overall decision-making, actual purchase behaviour (60.5%) indicates a stronger influence than self-reported awareness suggests.

__5.2 Conclusion__

This study set out to examine the impact of social media marketing on consumer purchase decisions, with primary data collected from 43 respondents in the Jhansi region. The findings confirm that social media marketing does have a meaningful and significant impact on consumer purchase decisions, though the strength and nature of this influence varies across different dimensions.

Social media has become an integral part of daily life for the majority of respondents, with 97.7% spending at least one hour per day on platforms such as Instagram and YouTube. The high frequency of ad exposure (88.4% encounter ads sometimes to very often) ensures that social media marketing reaches consumers consistently. The most powerful form of social media marketing, according to the data, is influencer and creator content, which 39.5% of respondents identify as the most influential content type. This is followed by customer reviews, brand pages, discount offers, and paid advertisements.

The most compelling evidence for the impact of social media marketing on purchase decisions comes from the finding that 60.5% of respondents have made an actual purchase after seeing a product on social media. When combined with the 32.6% who considered a purchase without completing it, an overwhelming 93.1% of respondents have been influenced to at least consider a purchase through social media. This demonstrates that social media marketing is a powerful driver of consumer purchase behaviour, even if consumers do not always consciously attribute their decisions to social media influence.

Regarding local businesses in Jhansi, the study finds that while most respondents consider local businesses to be using social media somewhat to very effectively (76.8%), the conversion rate from social media exposure to actual purchase from local businesses (39.5%) indicates a gap that needs to be addressed through better content quality, targeted advertising, and stronger calls to action.

The partial acceptance of H2 (trust in reviews) suggests that building consumer trust remains a challenge for brands and local businesses alike. The predominantly neutral response to trust (72.1%) indicates that consumers are discerning and do not blindly trust social media reviews. Brands must therefore invest in creating authentic, transparent, and verifiable social media content to build genuine consumer trust over time.

In conclusion, social media marketing significantly influences consumer purchase decisions in Jhansi, particularly through product discovery, price comparison facilitation, and actual purchase conversions. The study confirms the core hypothesis (H5) that social media platforms significantly influence consumer decision-making, as evidenced by the actual behavioural data showing 60.5% purchase rate via social media.

__5.3 Recommendations__

__5.3.1 For Local Businesses in Jhansi__

- Invest in Influencer Marketing: Since influencer/creator posts are the most influential content type (39.5%), local businesses should collaborate with local micro-influencers and content creators on Instagram and YouTube to promote their products. Micro-influencers with local relevance tend to have higher engagement rates and lower costs.
- Leverage Instagram and YouTube: As these are the most-used platforms in the sample, local businesses should prioritise creating high-quality, visually engaging content for Instagram and YouTube. Reels, short videos, and product demos tend to generate high engagement.
- Build Authenticity Through Reviews: Given that 72.1% of respondents are neutral about trusting social media reviews, businesses should actively encourage satisfied customers to post genuine reviews and testimonials. Verified reviews with photos and videos are significantly more trusted than text-only reviews.
- Improve Content Quality and Calls to Action: The gap between social media effectiveness (76.8% find local businesses somewhat/very effective) and actual purchase conversion (39.5%) suggests that while businesses are creating content, it may not be compelling enough to drive purchases. Clear calls to action, limited-time offers, and easy purchase links can improve conversion rates.
- Use Discount Offers Strategically: While discount offers and flash sales are only the fourth most influential content type overall (11.6%), they can be effective in converting undecided consumers. Periodic flash sales announced exclusively on social media can drive urgency and purchases.

__5.3.2 For Marketers and Policymakers__

- Develop Transparent Social Media Marketing Standards: Given the moderate trust in social media reviews (72.1% neutral), industry standards for transparent disclosure of paid partnerships and sponsored content would help build consumer trust in social media marketing overall.
- Promote Digital Marketing Training for Small Businesses: Local government and business associations in Jhansi should consider providing digital marketing training to small and medium businesses to help them create more effective social media marketing strategies.
- Focus on Mobile-First Content: As most social media access in India is through smartphones, all marketing content should be optimised for mobile viewing, with short videos, easy-to-read text, and fast-loading images.

__5.4 Limitations and Future Research__

This study has several limitations that should be acknowledged. First, the sample size of 43 respondents is relatively small and may not be fully representative of the entire social media user population in Jhansi. Future research should use a larger sample size (at least 150-200 respondents) to improve statistical reliability. Second, the study relied on self-reported data through an online questionnaire, which may be subject to social desirability bias and response bias. Third, the data was collected over a very short period (two days, 30-31 March 2026), which may not capture longer-term trends in consumer behaviour.

Fourth, the study did not apply advanced statistical techniques such as SPSS-based regression analysis or Structural Equation Modelling (SEM) due to the small sample size; future studies with larger samples should employ these tools. Fifth, the study was limited to Jhansi and may not be generalised to other Indian cities with different demographic and digital literacy profiles.

For future research, the following directions are suggested:

- Conduct a larger-scale study with 150-300 respondents from Jhansi and surrounding cities to enable regression and correlation analysis.
- Examine gender differences in social media marketing influence on purchase decisions, as the near-equal gender split in this sample provides a basis for comparative analysis.
- Study the specific impact of influencer marketing by local micro-influencers on purchase decisions for local Jhansi businesses.
- Conduct a longitudinal study to examine how social media marketing influence on purchase decisions changes over time, particularly as digital penetration increases in smaller Indian cities.
- Explore the role of WhatsApp marketing (which is widely used in this sample) as a distinct channel for local business marketing in Indian cities.
